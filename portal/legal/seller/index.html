Terms and Conditions

Last Updated: 4 June 2025

Welcome to the Mzansi Marketplace Seller Program. By registering as a Seller, you agree to these Terms and Conditions, forming a legally binding agreement with Mzansi Marketplace ("Platform"), a South African registered company. These terms govern your use of the Platform for listing, selling, and managing transactions.

We comply with the Consumer Protection Act (CPA) No. 68 of 2008, Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) No. 4 of 2013, and Electronic Communications and Transactions Act (ECTA) No. 25 of 2002.

1. Seller Eligibility

You must be at least 18 years old and provide accurate business and personal details during registration, including ID verification. Misrepresentation leads to immediate termination.

2. Seller Responsibilities

You are responsible for:

  • Accurate and honest product listings, per National Consumer Commission v Takealot (2021), avoiding misleading claims.
  • Ensuring products meet safety and quality standards under the CPA.
  • Processing, fulfilling, and delivering orders promptly.
  • Handling returns and refunds per your policy, compliant with CPA Section 56.
  • Complying with POPIA when handling buyer data, obtaining consent before sharing.

3. Payments and Fees

Payments are processed via Yoco, with funds held until withdrawal. You’ll link a valid bank account for payouts, subject to commission fees based on your Subscription Plan (viewable in your Dashboard). Withdrawals are secure, with AES-256 encryption, and processed within 72 hours.

4. Platform Role

The Platform is an intermediary, not responsible for product quality, fulfillment, or delivery disputes. We facilitate communication and process payments but are not liable for seller-buyer disputes.

5. Termination

We may terminate your account for violations like fraud or misleading listings. You may terminate by contacting support, settling pending orders. Data is retained for 5 years post-termination per the Tax Administration Act.

6. Dispute Resolution

Resolve disputes with buyers directly. If unresolved, escalate to support for mediation under CPA. Unresolved disputes may proceed to the Arbitration Foundation of South Africa (AFSA). Contact the Consumer Goods and Services Ombud at info@cgsosa.co.za.

7. Amendments

We may update these terms, notifying you via email or Dashboard with 30 days’ notice per CPA. Continued use constitutes acceptance.

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 4 June 2025

We are committed to protecting your privacy as a Seller, in compliance with POPIA No. 4 of 2013. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal information.

1. Information We Collect

We collect:

  • Personal details (name, email, ID number, selfie) for registration and verification.
  • Financial information (bank details) for payouts, encrypted with AES-256.
  • Transaction data (listings, sales, withdrawals).
  • Usage data (IP address, browser type) via cookies, per ECTA.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your data to:

  • Manage your Seller account and process payouts.
  • Facilitate transactions and resolve disputes.
  • Improve Platform functionality through analytics.
  • Comply with legal obligations (e.g., SARS reporting).
By registering, you consent to this processing. Opt out of marketing via Dashboard settings.

3. Data Sharing

We share data with:

  • Buyers (delivery details for order fulfillment).
  • Yoco for payment processing, under PCI DSS compliance.
  • Authorities if legally required (e.g., SARS).
No data is sold or shared for marketing without consent.

4. Data Security

We use AES-256 encryption, secure access controls, and annual audits to protect your data. In the 2022 Dis-Chem case, poor security led to breaches, reinforcing our commitment to robust safeguards. Breaches are reported within 72 hours per POPIA, with steps to secure your account.

5. Data Retention

Data is retained as needed (e.g., 7 years for tax records) and securely deleted thereafter, per the Tax Administration Act.

6. Your Rights

Under POPIA, you can:

  • Access or correct your data.
  • Request deletion, subject to legal retention.
  • Object to non-essential processing.
  • Request data portability.
Contact support@themzansimarketplace.co.za.

7. Cookies

We use first-party cookies for functionality and analytics. Manage preferences via browser settings or our cookie consent tool.

Customer Agreement

Last Updated: 4 June 2025

This Customer Agreement governs your interactions with buyers as a Seller on Mzansi Marketplace, ensuring fair and transparent transactions.

1. Buyer Interactions

You agree to:

  • Provide clear, accurate product descriptions and pricing, per CPA.
  • Communicate promptly with buyers regarding orders and issues.
  • Offer fair return and refund policies, compliant with CPA Section 56.

2. Dispute Handling

Address buyer disputes directly within 5 business days. Escalate unresolved issues to Platform support for mediation. Misuse of disputes (e.g., false claims) may lead to account suspension.

3. Buyer Data

Handle buyer personal information (e.g., delivery details) per POPIA, ensuring confidentiality and obtaining consent for use. Non-compliance, as seen in Information Regulator v SAPS (2023), may result in penalties.

4. Liability

You are liable for product quality, delivery, and post-sale service. The Platform is not responsible for buyer disputes but will assist with communication.
